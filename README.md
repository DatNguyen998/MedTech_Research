# 🏥 MedTech Market Research & Insights

Hello and welcome. This repository is a quiet space dedicated to my ongoing research, notes, and observations regarding the Medical Technology (MedTech) and Healthcare IT industry. 

---

### 👤 About the Author
My name is **Kent Nguyen**. I am a Business Analyst who enjoys exploring how complex systems can be simplified to help people. I am generally a quiet person, but I have a deep and growing curiosity about how digital transformation intersects with human health. In my professional work, I have helped implement systems that support healthcare operations, which inspired me to look closer at the broader industry.

---

### 🌱 The Purpose of This Space
I started this project primarily for **learning and observation**. 
Healthcare is an area where technology has the profound ability to improve, and even save, lives. Through this repository, I am trying to understand:
* How healthcare providers adopt new technologies (like ERPs, patient management systems, and AI).
* The workflows and pain points that doctors, nurses, and administrators face daily.
* The current market trends, innovations, and regulatory landscapes shaping MedTech, especially in developing markets.

I believe that to build truly helpful digital solutions, we first need to sit down and understand the human challenges behind them.

---

### 🗂️ What I Am Gathering Here
As I read, learn, and analyze, I plan to gradually organize this repository into:
* **Industry Overviews:** Summaries of key MedTech sectors (e.g., Telemedicine, Hospital Information Systems, Wearables).
* **Process Mapping:** Visualizing standard healthcare workflows (like patient intake or inventory management) using tools like Figma or Draw.io.
* **System Analysis:** Case studies on how platforms like Odoo or SAP are adapted for clinical environments.
* **Data & Trends:** Small analytical observations (using Python and SQL) on market growth and technology adoption rates.

---

### 🛠️ The Toolkit
This is a research-focused repository, so the tools are geared towards analysis and documentation:
* **Data Processing:** Python & SQL (for structuring any market data I find).
* **Documentation:** Markdown (for clean, readable notes).
* **Process Design:** BPMN / UML mapping to understand clinical workflows.
